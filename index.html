About

Career Aspirations


Throughout my life I have been fascinated by the ability of computers to automate mundane processes, and have more recently been an avid follower of bleeding-edge tech in the software space. Whether it be shaping stronger economies, safer roads, or more productive lives, software products and algorithms have been immensely successful when focused toward unmet needs. As the driving force behind these advancements, software developers apply engineering principles and technology to develop programs for computers and user-focused applications.


Due to the advent of modern processing capacity, software engineers have seen an uptake in market demand during the past several years. Programming knowledge is vital to software development, with specialty in Python and its broad range of Machine Learning libraries a highly sought after skill. Additionally, familiarity in front-end and scripting languages such as JavaScript and PHP along with Microsoft Office tools such as Access contribute to the development of intuitive interfaces and robust back-end systems.


Although the high competition for software engineering positions is a notorious obstacle to those looking to switch careers midway through their studies, there is a steadily growing number of transfer employees. According to the MIT Technology Review, the majority of Computer Scientists hold degrees in CS or CE, but up to 20% who pursue non-computing STEM degrees now work as software developers. Many of these migrations can be attributed to Computer Science’s rising appeal as a market opportunity and steady source of income.


Since 2010, there has been a 59% leap in jobs for software developers—and a 15% jump in salary, to an average of $107,510 in 2019—according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). Sitting comfortably above the national median salary of $34,248 and requiring only a Bachelor’s degree for entry-level jobs, it is clear why careers in software development have garnered such a growing interest.
